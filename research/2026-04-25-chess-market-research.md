## V. History of Chess (Brand Anchors)

### Ancient Roots (6th–10th c.)
- **India (Chaturanga, 6th c.):** "four divisions" — infantry, cavalry, elephants, chariots → pawns, knights, bishops, rooks
- **Persia (Shatranj, ~600 CE):** "Shah!" (King) and "Shah Mat!" (King is helpless) → "checkmate"
- **Arab world:** Islamic conquest spread the game; pieces abstracted from realistic figures to abstract shapes due to religious bans on idols

### Medieval/Renaissance Europe (10th–16th c.)
- Reached Europe by 1000 CE via Moorish Spain + Byzantine Empire
- "Royal Game" — favored by Richard I, Ivan the Terrible
- Late 15th c.: "Mad Queen Chess" — counselor became Queen with sweeping movement (likely inspired by powerful female monarchs)
- Modern rules (castling, pawn 2-square initial move) standardized

### Modern Era (19th c.–present)
- 1851: First international tournament (London) — won by Adolf Anderssen
- 1849: Staunton pattern set designed (still global standard)
- 1886: Wilhelm Steinitz becomes first official World Champion (def. Zukertort)
- **1997: Deep Blue defeats Kasparov** — pivotal moment for computer chess
- Today: Stockfish + Chess.com platform-driven era

**Brand anchors for Hokage marketing copy:**
- "Shah Mat" → checkmate etymology = "King is helpless" — useful for underdog positioning
- "Mad Queen Chess" — disruption of the established order via a single piece's empowerment (mirrors Hokage's "earned through grit" framing)
- Deep Blue moment → why human-led teaching still matters in the Stockfish era (engines analyze, humans teach the journey)
