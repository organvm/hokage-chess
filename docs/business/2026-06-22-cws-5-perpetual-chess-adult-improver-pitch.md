## 4. The Story Spine (what makes Rob bookable)

A bookable adult-improver pitch needs a *hook*, not a résumé. Rob's hook is that he is the only person in the genre running an explicitly **named narrative architecture** over a real rating climb. The angles, strongest first:

1. **The named structure.** Rob builds every episode on **Ki-Sho-Ten-Ketsu** (the four-act kishōtenketsu narrative structure). Almost no chess creator articulates the storytelling craft under "Road to X" content. Naming it is itself the differentiator — and it makes for a genuinely novel podcast conversation about *how you turn improvement into narrative*.
2. **The Hokage / shōnen frame.** A Naruto-themed, shōnen-native chess channel is, by current evidence, structurally unique in chess. The "training arc," "missions," and "rank-up exams" language maps the hero's-journey skeleton onto real improvement work. This is a fresh angle for a show that has interviewed 100+ improvers.
3. **The real climb.** ~1350 ELO climbing toward 1500, 322 videos, a public "Road to 1500" arc. Honest losses and post-game corrections on camera — not grandmaster theater.
4. **The outer-borough NYC voice.** Staten Island / working-class adult-improver positioning, Rocky-underdog energy. NYC chess media skews Manhattan/Marshall; Rob's lane is open.
5. **Process over talent.** Rob is a documentation-discipline case study (322 videos, consistent ritual) more than a talent case study — which is the *more useful* story for the show's audience of working adults trying to improve around a job.

**One-line positioning (use verbatim as the pitch's spine):**
> A working adult in Staten Island who turned his climb from 1350 toward 1500 into an explicitly Naruto-structured "training arc" — 322 videos built on the Ki-Sho-Ten-Ketsu narrative model — and what naming the story did for the discipline of improving.

## 7. Follow-Up & Persistence Protocol

The world map is explicit that the bottleneck is *persistence*, not access. Most pitches fail by going silent, not by being rejected. Cadence:

| Touch | Timing | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Send | Week 0 | Variant A to Perpetual Chess + Chess Journeys variant to Chess Journeys, same week. |
| Follow-up 1 | +2 weeks, no reply | One short, friendly bump. Add one *new* fact (a milestone hit, a recent video, a rating tick). Never re-paste the original. |
| Warm signal | Ongoing | Engage genuinely with the host's posts/episodes (thoughtful reply, share an episode). No DMs that read as transactional. |
| Re-pitch | +1 quarter, no reply | Re-send with a stronger hook: a rating milestone (crossing 1400/1500), a notable video, or a Chess Journeys appearance as a new credit. |
| Stop condition | After 3 declined or unanswered cycles over ~6 months | Park it. Revisit when a hard milestone lands (1500 reached, a viral video, a Chessable course). A bigger story makes the next pitch a different email. |

**Rule:** every follow-up must carry a new fact. A bump with nothing new added is noise; a bump that says "since I wrote, I crossed 1400 and shipped my 350th video" is a fresh, bookable signal.

## 8. Measurement

This atom is "done" at the system level when the pitch package exists (below). Track the external outcome separately:

- **Sent:** date the first email went out (binary; the only metric Rob controls).
- **Reply:** any response from a host.
- **Booked:** a recording scheduled.
- **Aired:** episode published — the actual credential event.
- **Downstream:** newsletter signups / channel subs attributable to the appearance window (use a dedicated CTA or signup tag so the spillover is countable).

Sent is the only one Rob owns outright; the rest are out of his hands. Optimize for *sent + persisted*, not for *booked* — booking is a function of repeated, improving pitches over time.
