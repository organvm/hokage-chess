## 1. Why "Bridge" content?

The Hokage premium-content reserves (322 videos) are deep but undifferentiated from the perspective of the algorithm. The Bridge Content pillar adds **two recurring formats** that:

1. **Carry algorithmic gravity** — recurring formats train YouTube/Twitter recommendation surfaces to associate Hokage with a specific *thing* viewers can subscribe to.
2. **Map directly to funnel levels** — every Bridge piece carries a CTA to the L2 lead magnet (`/free-plan` route, "Get to 1400" repertoire PDF). Bridge content is the on-ramp.
3. **Express the brand DNA** — Naruto framing (Jutsu) + Rocky-underdog framing (Boss Battle) are pre-built Hokage idioms. Templates lean into already-decided positioning.

Bridge is **the pillar between premium-content production (existing) and funnel capture (next)**. Without Bridge, the existing 322 videos remain a library without a turnstile.

## 2. Template 1 — Jutsu of the Week

**Premise:** every chess concept is a "jutsu" — a named technique with conditions, motion, and counter. Each Jutsu of the Week is a single named technique, deeply taught, with a memorable name.

**Length:** 8-12 minutes (long-form, YouTube-native)
**Cadence:** weekly, posted Tuesday (lands in Welcome Wednesday Discord ritual — see PRT-041)
**Channel:** YouTube primary; 60-90s Shorts re-cut for IG Reels + TikTok + YouTube Shorts cross-post

### 2.1 Episode structure (every Jutsu video)

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:08 | **Hook** — show the position where the jutsu wins, before naming it | Algorithmic retention (8-second rule) |
| 0:08-0:30 | **Title card + jutsu name** — "Today's jutsu: [Name]" | Brand stamp, search-friendly |
| 0:30-2:00 | **The trigger conditions** — when does this jutsu apply? When does it not? | Educational frame |
| 2:00-7:00 | **The execution** — Rob walks through 2-3 example positions (his own games preferred — climb authority) | Core teaching |
| 7:00-9:00 | **The counter** — what happens when the opponent knows it? | Nuance, not just hype |
| 9:00-10:00 | **The drill** — homework: a 5-puzzle Lichess study (linked) so viewer can practice | Action conversion (study link is L1→L2 micro-step) |
| 10:00-end | **CTA** — "Want my full opening repertoire? Free PDF at hokagechess.com/free-plan" | L2 capture |

### 2.2 Jutsu naming convention

Names should be **memorable + functional**:

- ✅ "The Silent Pin" (a pin that doesn't visually announce itself)
- ✅ "The Anti-Italian Counter-Slap" (a specific Black response)
- ✅ "The Endgame Tempo Steal" (using zugzwang)
- ❌ "How to use pins" (generic, search-saturated)
- ❌ "Chess tip #43" (no brand, no memory)

Each name doubles as **a Hokage canon term** — over time, Genin Squad members refer to "the Tempo Steal" as shorthand. The jutsu lexicon becomes proprietary.

### 2.4 Production friction — minimum-viable workflow

To sustain weekly cadence:
1. Pull jutsu position from Rob's own games (chess.com archive — already scraped by `research/` folder).
2. Sketch the 7-beat structure in 15 minutes (template above).
3. Record screen + voiceover on chess.com Analysis board (45 minutes raw).
4. Edit to 8-12min in DaVinci/Final Cut (60-90 minutes).
5. Cross-post Shorts cut (15 minutes).

**Total per episode:** ~3 hours including research. **Sustainable cadence floor:** 1 per week.

## 3. Template 2 — Boss Battle

**Premise:** every month, Rob plays (or analyzes) a clearly-stronger opponent and breaks down the encounter. Mirror of how shonen anime arcs work — the protagonist faces a named, established antagonist; the audience watches the climb close in real time.

**Length:** 15-25 minutes (mid-form, designed for YouTube watch-time signal)
**Cadence:** monthly, posted final Friday (algorithmic surge from Loot Drop Friday Discord ritual — PRT-041)
**Channel:** YouTube primary; **NO** Shorts cut (long-form depth is the format's whole point)

### 3.1 Episode structure (every Boss Battle video)

| Time | Beat | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:30 | **Cold open** — the *one moment* the Boss made Rob feel cornered | Hook + emotional stakes |
| 0:30-2:00 | **Boss intro** — who is this opponent? rating, reputation, known strengths | Stakes-setting |
| 2:00-4:00 | **Pre-game prep** — what did Rob know about this opponent? what was the strategy? | Authentic-climb authority |
| 4:00-15:00 | **The game** — annotated, with Rob's *real-time* mental state interleaved | Teaching + drama |
| 15:00-19:00 | **The pivot moment** — the move that decided the game (won or lost) | Climactic structure |
| 19:00-22:00 | **What Rob would do differently** — honest post-mortem | Vulnerability + growth = trust |
| 22:00-24:00 | **What the viewer takes home** — 3 transferable lessons from the encounter | Practical conversion |
| 24:00-end | **CTA** — "Want to climb with me? Free repertoire at hokagechess.com/free-plan + Genin Squad info" | L2 + L3 capture |

### 3.2 Boss roster (proposed)

| Tier | Boss type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Recurring** | "The Abominable Chessman" — Rob's existing in-channel rival (already canon) | Existing series — extend |
| **Park Boss** | NYC chess-park stronger player Rob plays in person | Washington Square / Bryant Park regulars (~1700-2000 USCF) |
| **Online Boss** | chess.com / Lichess opponent at +200 ELO over Rob | Real games from Rob's existing archive |
| **Title Boss** | titled player (NM/FM/IM) Rob plays in a casual stream | Stretch goal — book guests |
| **Echo Boss** | a past version of Rob — analyze his own game from 2 years ago | Lineage device, no scheduling needed |

The roster mixes **scheduled wins** (Park, Echo) with **stretch wins** (Title) so cadence is sustainable even if booking falls through.

### 3.3 The "boss battle" archetype as Hokage canon

Each named Boss becomes a recurring character in the Hokage cinematic universe. The Abominable Chessman is canon (existing). Future Bosses get equivalent treatment — name, recurring appearances, established strengths/weaknesses. **The chess channel as serialized narrative.**

This is the bridge to Apex Predator parent architecture's "named-rival" pattern: Rob has been doing this instinctively in BODI competitive contexts; making it explicit in Hokage formalizes it.

## 4. Cross-format CTA discipline

Every Bridge piece (Jutsu OR Boss Battle) carries the same CTA structure:

1. **Primary CTA** — `hokagechess.com/free-plan` (L2 lead magnet capture)
2. **Secondary CTA** — Hokage Discord invite (drives PRT-041 ritual participation)
3. **Tertiary CTA** — subscribe + bell (channel flywheel)

CTAs do *not* drift between videos. **One CTA per slot, always the same.** This is BODI's L2 mechanism applied to Hokage's content surface — every piece points the same direction, so the funnel can measure conversion.

## 5. KPIs (per episode, per format)

| Metric | Jutsu target | Boss Battle target | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8-sec retention | ≥70% | ≥70% | YouTube Studio |
| Average view duration | ≥45% | ≥40% | YouTube Studio |
| `/free-plan` click-through (per video link) | ≥1.5% | ≥3% | Kit + UTM tracking |
| Discord new joins (next 72h post-publish) | ≥3 | ≥10 | Discord audit log |
| Genin Squad sign-ups (next 7d post-publish) | ≥1 | ≥3 | Stripe + Kit tag |

**Floor (drop the format if persistently below):**
- Jutsu: <50% 8-sec retention OR <0.5% CTA click-through
- Boss Battle: <60% 8-sec retention OR <1% CTA click-through

**Ceiling (scale up cadence if persistently above):**
- Jutsu: ≥80% retention AND ≥3% CTA → consider 2/wk
- Boss Battle: ≥75% retention AND ≥5% CTA → consider biweekly
