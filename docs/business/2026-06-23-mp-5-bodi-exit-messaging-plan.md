# MP-5: BODI Exit Messaging Plan

**Atom:** MP-5  
**Date:** 2026-06-23  
**Issue:** `4444J99/hokage-chess#38`  
**Source:** `2026-04-25-bodi-reformation-spec.md`, `2026-04-26-rob-atomized-todos.md`  
**North Star:** Exit the BODI distribution model with dignity and continuity so no key relationship burns.

## 1) Objective

Create a respectful communication sequence that:

- Keeps brand relationships intact.
- Supports existing customers and downline transition.
- Gives a clear on-ramp to the new owned model.

## 2) Timing gates (what must be live first)

Do not announce publicly until all three conditions are true:

1. **FWS-3** (`Legion Bulk Protocol`) is released and sale-capable.
2. **FWS-2** (`Skool/Discord/Whop` gate) is provisioned and onboarding-ready.
3. The owned coaching/fitness funnel (L1-L4) is live on Rob-owned infrastructure.

If any requirement is missing, keep all messaging private and preparatory.

## 3) Sequencing (inner circle -> outer circle)

1. **Beachbody corporate lead / direct upline** (private, first).
2. **Active downline leaders / referral network** (private calls + group note next).
3. **Existing BODI customers** (VIP and active clients first, then broad customer segment).
4. **Public audience** (email + socials).

Rationale: protect relationship-sensitive groups first to avoid rumors and emotional volatility.

## 4) Talk tracks and framing language

### A) Beachbody Corp / direct upline

**Framing:** gratitude, professionalism, no criticism.

> "Hey [Name], I wanted to share this with you first. I am transitioning my focus away from actively building the BODI business and toward a fully owner-led fitness coaching model. This is not a criticism of BODI — I have deep respect for the foundation and opportunities it gave me."  
> "I’ll keep this transition disciplined and professional and wanted you to hear it directly from me so we avoid any confusion."

### B) Downline / referral network

**Framing:** transparency, choice, shared ownership.

> "Team, I’m moving into a full owner-led phase with my own coaching and community system. I will no longer be actively recruiting or building the BODI downline."  
> "You remain important regardless of your path forward. If you want to transition, I’ll give you direct clarity on what is changing and what remains available. If you stay in BODI, keep doing what you do well; I support your path."

### C) Existing customers (VIPs, paid clients, active users)

**Framing:** continuity, upgraded support options, clear migration choice.

> "Thanks for staying with me. I’m entering the next phase by moving coaching, plans, and community to my owned platform so I can serve people more directly."  
> "Your current support commitments stay respected, and I’ll keep continuity through transition. For those who want, I’ll share the onboarding path to the new community and priority slots."

### D) Public audience

**Framing:** evolution, not rejection.

> "BODI helped me build the foundation for this business, and I’m grateful for that. The next chapter is my own owned fitness coaching ecosystem, where I can build and support people more directly."  
> "If you’ve followed this journey and want more depth, more accountability, and a stronger direct community, this is the next chapter."

## 5) Rollout mechanics (timing windows)

- **T-3 days:** finalize transition page, FAQ, and support response script.
- **T-1 day:** queue personal notes for corp/upline and downline.
- **T0:** send corporate/upline private notice, then downline notice same day.
- **T+1:** VIP/customer one-to-one or segmented outreach.
- **T+2 to T+3:** broad customer email + social announcement.

## 6) Guardrails (prevent burning bridges)

- No negative mentions of BODI.
- No abrupt cutoff language.
- Keep legacy support and legacy customer access clear for a transition window.
- Provide one canonical FAQ and one canonical landing page.
- Keep every message short, grateful, and specific on what stays the same vs what changes.

## 7) Risks and mitigations

- **Risk:** downline feels abandoned.
  - **Mitigation:** early private outreach; explicit option-based framing; continued mentoring access in transition path.
- **Risk:** customer churn from abrupt change.
  - **Mitigation:** phase-based transition + clear continuity promises + support handoff map.
- **Risk:** reputational hit from perceived rejection.
  - **Mitigation:** keep tone appreciative and forward-looking; no blame language anywhere.
