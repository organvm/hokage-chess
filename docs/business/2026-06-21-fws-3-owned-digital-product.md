## 1. Decision: Selected Product

Between the two candidates:
- **Candidate A:** 'Body Beast Companion Guide' (leverages program he already runs)
- **Candidate B:** 'Legion Beginner Bulk Protocol' (productizes informal coaching)

**Decision:** **Legion Beginner Bulk Protocol**
**Why:** The 'Legion Beginner Bulk Protocol' provides a completely independent, owned asset with Rob's name on the cover. While a companion guide to Body Beast still ties the user indirectly back to the BODI ecosystem (requiring them to own or buy Body Beast), the Legion Bulk Protocol is standalone. It captures the intermediate-to-advanced beginner audience entirely within Rob's funnel, pointing directly at his checkout and branding.

## 2. Product Spec: Legion Beginner Bulk Protocol

*   **Format:** Digital PDF Program + Notion Template (Tracking).
*   **Price Point:** $27 (Entry-level, low friction).
*   **Target Audience:** Intermediate lifters (hypertrophy-curious) who have stalled on beginner routines and need a structured, 12-week bulk.
*   **Platform / Fulfillment:** Whop or Skool (integrated with FWS-2).
*   **Contents:**
    *   **Phase 1: The Philosophy:** Why traditional bulks fail (dirty bulking vs. lean bulking), and the mental discipline required.
    *   **Phase 2: The Nutrition Protocol:** Macros, meal timing, and a sample grocery list (linking back to his nutrition habits).
    *   **Phase 3: The 12-Week Programming:**
        *   Weeks 1-4: Foundation & Volume.
        *   Weeks 5-8: Progressive Overload & Intensity.
        *   Weeks 9-12: Peaking & Hypertrophy Focus.
    *   **Phase 4: Recovery:** Sleep tracking and readiness (ties into FWS-6 wearable affiliate integration).
*   **Upsell (The Funnel):**
    *   **Primary:** "Need more accountability? Join the Legion of Fitness Paid Community." (FWS-2)
    *   **Secondary:** "Apply for 1:1 TrueCoach programming." (FWS-5)

## 3. Launch Strategy
