## 1. Executive Summary

MP-8 resolves the content side of the wearable question. FWS-6 still handles the affiliate application, code, and link. This plan defines when Rob films himself wearing the device, what the recurring content formats are, and how the data connects chess, fitness, and recovery without turning the channel into gadget reviews.

The move:

1. Pick **Whoop** as the first wearable because its strain, recovery, and sleep language maps cleanly to Rob's bridge thesis: physical load, cognitive load, and recovery all need programming.
2. Film it first as a visible tool inside the **FWS-4 Episode 3 recovery episode**, after a short baseline period.
3. Turn the wearable into a recurring operating layer:
   - Tuesday/Thursday fitness recovery programming.
   - Saturday bridge experiments around chess focus and fatigue.
   - Sunday Quest Log scorecards for owned-media continuity.

This is not "Rob reviews Whoop." The content angle is: **Rob trains by readiness, not ego.**

## 2. Wearable Decision

### Primary: Whoop

Use **Whoop** as the V1 wearable for content and programming.

Why:

| Criterion | Whoop fit |
|---|---|
| Fitness-programming language | Strong. Strain, sleep, and recovery are easy to explain inside training decisions. |
| Visual content | Strong. Wrist strap is visible in gym footage, desk footage, and chess B-roll. |
| Bridge content | Strong. "Load -> recovery -> next session" transfers naturally from lifting to chess study. |
| Affiliate/sponsor fit | Stronger than Oura for podcast/creator style integrations per the existing strategy notes. |
| Audience legibility | High. "Recovery score changed today's plan" is easier to understand than a broad wellness stack. |

### Fallback: Oura

Use Oura only if Rob strongly prefers a ring form factor, dislikes wearing a strap while filming, or wants a sleep-first/lifestyle-first aesthetic. Oura is credible, but its V1 role is less useful for Rob because MP-8 needs a visible training and programming prop, not only a passive sleep tracker.

### Rule

Do not split the stack in V1. One wearable, one vocabulary, one repeated scorecard. Add a second device only after 90 days of content data proves the audience cares.

## 4. Launch Sequence

### Week 0: Acquire and baseline

Rob gets the Whoop, applies through the affiliate path in Form 11, and wears it for a quiet baseline period.

Content captured:
- unboxing or setup B-roll,
- gym wrist shots,
- chess-desk wrist shots,
- one private note per day: sleep, recovery/readiness, training done, chess focus, subjective energy.

Do not publish a strong claim yet. The first week is calibration and footage gathering.

### Weeks 1-2: Passive visibility

The device appears in normal footage without becoming the headline.

Where it can appear:
- Tuesday fitness clip: "Today's plan is adjusted because recovery is low."
- Thursday recovery clip: "The plan changed, not the goal."
- Chess desk B-roll: visible strap while Rob reviews a game or solves tactics.

The audience should see the tool before being asked to care about it.

### Week 3: First explicit wearable episode

Anchor the explicit launch in **FWS-4 Episode 3: The Recovery Equation**.

Working title:
> I Changed My Workout Because My Recovery Was Bad

Hook:
> You do not grow in the gym. You grow when you recover. Today the data says the ego plan is wrong.

Structure:
1. Morning recovery check.
2. Original training plan.
3. Adjustment: reduce load, change volume, or shift to active recovery.
4. Chess bridge: when recovery is low, do not play hero chess. Train fundamentals, review losses, or run low-tilt puzzles.
5. CTA: Quest Log first; affiliate link only if FWS-6 is ready and disclosed.

### Weeks 4-7: Recurring format test

Run four weeks of repeated lightweight formats:
- two fitness clips per week,
- one bridge post every other Saturday,
- one Sunday Quest Log scorecard.

### Week 8: First lab recap

Publish a bridge recap:
> 30 Days of Training by Recovery: What Changed in My Gym and Chess

This is the first major MP-8 proof artifact. It should compare decisions and outcomes, not sell the device.

## 5. Recurring Content Formats

### A. Tuesday/Thursday: Recovery Programming Clips

**Pillar:** Fitness / Legion
**Length:** 45-90 seconds
**Cadence:** One or two per week
**Slot:** MP-2 Tuesday/Thursday fitness lanes

Template:
1. Show recovery/readiness context.
2. State the planned workout.
3. State the adjustment.
4. Explain the principle.
5. End with the fitness CTA.

Examples:
- "Low recovery, high discipline: how I modified leg day."
- "Green day: when the data says push."
- "The hardest fitness skill is not doing more."

### B. Saturday: Board Load Lab

**Pillar:** Bridge / Discipline
**Length:** short post, 60-90s clip, or biweekly major bridge artifact
**Cadence:** Every other Saturday to match MP-2

The frame is not that a wearable directly measures chess skill. The frame is that sleep, recovery, and accumulated strain affect the load Rob chooses for cognitive training.

Template:
1. Recovery state.
2. Chess load chosen: puzzles, rapid games, game review, opening study, or rest.
3. Outcome: blunders, time trouble, focus rating, tilt level, or lesson learned.
4. Transfer principle: program mental load the way you program physical load.

Examples:
- "I stopped playing rated games on red recovery days."
- "High strain yesterday, worse calculation today?"
- "Rest days are training days if you review instead of rage-queue."

### C. Sunday: Quest Log Wearable Scorecard

**Pillar:** Owned media / MP-7 segmented newsletter
**Length:** one compact section inside the Quest Log
**Cadence:** Weekly once the wearable is live

Scorecard fields:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Recovery trend | Green/yellow/red pattern or plain-language readiness trend. |
| Training decision | What changed in gym programming because of the signal. |
| Chess decision | What changed in chess training because of the signal. |
| Outcome | One concrete result: completed session, avoided tilt, adjusted volume, better sleep, etc. |
| Lesson | One transferable principle. |

The scorecard is for continuity. It should not become a medical diary.

### D. Monthly: Training Arc Lab Recap

**Pillar:** Bridge / long-form or newsletter
**Cadence:** Monthly

One self-experiment per month:
- 30 days of no rated chess on low-recovery mornings.
- 30 days of sleep-first workout programming.
- 30 days of late-night blitz replaced by game review.
- 30 days of deload discipline: gym and chess.

Each recap needs:
1. hypothesis,
2. constraint,
3. four-week result,
4. what Rob changes next.

## 6. Chess and Cognitive Load Angle

Use the phrase **cognitive load** carefully. Rob is not claiming Whoop measures chess cognition. The content claim is narrower and defensible:

> Recovery data helps Rob decide what kind of chess load to attempt.

### Chess load ladder

| Recovery/readiness state | Recommended chess load | Why |
|---|---|---|
| High | Rated rapid/classical, hard calculation sets, Boss Battle prep | Higher tolerance for deep focus and frustration. |
| Medium | Puzzles, opening review, one focused game, annotated review | Enough load to improve without forcing max pressure. |
| Low | Loss review, endgame basics, low-stakes puzzles, rest | Protects against tilt spirals and garbage volume. |

### Trackable chess outcomes

Use simple signals:
- number of rated games,
- puzzle attempts and accuracy,
- self-rated focus from 1-5,
- tilt events,
- time trouble,
- blunder count or engine-review accuracy when available.

Do not overfit. The point is repeatable decision-making, not proving a scientific relationship.

## 7. Fitness and Recovery Programming Angle

The fitness side is the cleaner use case. Rob can use Whoop as a visible decision layer for recovery-aware programming.

### Programming rules

| Signal | Training action | Content angle |
|---|---|---|
| High recovery | Push planned intensity if form is clean. | "Earned intensity." |
| Medium recovery | Run the plan, but cap ego lifts and watch rest periods. | "Discipline is staying inside the plan." |
| Low recovery | Reduce volume, shift to technique, mobility, walking, or rest. | "Recovery is not weakness." |
| High strain week | Plan deload or active recovery. | "You cannot PR every arc." |
| Sleep debt | Lower cognitive + physical demand. | "The body keeps receipts." |

### Coaching tie-in

For clients or cohort members, wearable data remains optional. If a member shares data, Rob can use it to guide check-ins:
- "What did sleep look like this week?"
- "Did training load match recovery?"
- "Where did you force intensity when the plan called for restraint?"

No participant should be required to publish screenshots of biometric data.

---

## 8. Data, Privacy, and Claim Boundaries

These rules are mandatory:

1. **No medical claims.** Say "readiness signal" or "consumer recovery data," not diagnosis.
2. **No public client biometrics without explicit consent.** Redact screenshots by default.
3. **No shame framing.** Low recovery is information, not moral failure.
4. **No fake precision.** A score can inform a decision; it does not prove why a game was won or lost.
5. **Affiliate disclosure every time.** Once FWS-6 has a code, any content using that code needs a clear affiliate disclosure.
6. **Human override stays visible.** Rob can say: "The score says X, my body says Y, so I am choosing Z."

The trust asset is not the device. The trust asset is Rob showing how he makes disciplined decisions with imperfect data.
