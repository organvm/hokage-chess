# Trinary Review: Pipeline Go-To-Market Spec
**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Reviewing:** `docs/business/2026-04-27-pipeline-go-to-market.md`
**Reviewers:** The Absorber, The Critic, The Contrarian

---

## 1. The Absorber (Expander / Net Positives)
*   **The "Demo Application" Loop:** This is the strongest part of the spec. By transforming a boring application into a functional system demo, you effectively turn a Cost Center (job hunting) into a Lead Generation engine.
*   **Expansion Opportunity:** We should expand the "Aerarium" pillar to include a "Knowledge Dividend." Every time the pipeline successfully researches a company, that research is indexed as an asset that can be sold or reused in the Consultancy pillar.
*   **Net Positive:** The system builds equity even in rejection. A "No" from a company still leaves us with a deep-target research profile and a demo on their radar.

## 2. The Critic (Logic Gaps / Complexity Risk)
*   **Pillar Conflict:** The "Aerarium" and "Consultancy" pillars might cannibalize each other. If we sell the "Consultancy" (Human Layer), we are admitting the "Pipeline" (System) isn't 100% effective on its own.
*   **Complexity Trap:** The "Avalanche Handoff Protocol" is elegant but brittle. If a recruiter uses a non-standard portal (Workday/Lever) that blocks the "Self-Unraveling Narrative," the entire conversion loop breaks.
*   **Missing Metric:** There is no mention of "Context-to-Conversion" ratio. We need a way to measure if the "rich aesthetics" of the demo actually increase interview rates or just scare off traditional HR.

## 3. The Contrarian (Human Logic / Real-World Friction)
*   **Premise Challenge:** Most high-end applicants don't want a "viral loop." They want privacy. Signaling to one recruiter that you are using a "factory" to apply to 50 others might look desperate or "spammy" rather than efficient.
*   **The "Black Box" Problem:** Recruiters hate feeling like they're being "gamed" by an agent. If the application feels too polished or "automated," they will immediately look for the "Human in the Loop" to verify integrity.
*   **Consultancy Reality:** High-end consulting is a referral business, not a SaaS byproduct. The "Inverted Interview" might get you a meeting, but it won't close a $50k engagement without a separate, high-trust human sales cycle that isn't documented here.

---

## Synthesis & Hardening Requirements
1.  **Stealth Mode:** Add a "Privacy Filter" to the Pipeline to allow for high-intent, single-target applications that don't reveal the "factory" mechanics.
2.  **Hybrid Proof:** The "Testament" series must emphasize the human-curated prompts that drive the agents, proving it's an "Augmented Human" system, not just a bot farm.
3.  **Resilience Check:** Define a fallback for when the "Self-Unraveling Narrative" link cannot be included in the primary application.
