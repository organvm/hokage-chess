## Post Copy

**I stopped applying for jobs. I started building the factory that builds the applications.** 🏗️

Most high-end technical applications are a black hole of wasted context. You spend hours researching a company, tailoring a letter, and mapping your skills, only to have a recruiter scan it for 6 seconds.

I decided to invert the interview.

Over the last 48 hours, I've codified a "Pipeline" that:
1.  **Ingests the Target Universe:** Automatically maps a company's docs, product, and team using specialized research agents.
2.  **Enforces Temporal Integrity:** Rejects any opportunity older than 48 hours. If you're late to the sequence, you've already lost.
3.  **Deploys a Trinary Review:** Every application is stress-tested by three adversarial AI personas (The Absorber, The Critic, The Contrarian) to find logic gaps before a human ever sees it.
4.  **Creates a "Self-Unraveling Narrative":** Instead of a static PDF, the application is a living system demo. The hiring manager doesn't just see what I've done—they see the system I built to find them.

The result? The interview isn't about my past. It's a demo of my current infrastructure.

**If my application is this efficient because of the tools I built, what could your entire engineering team do with those same tools?**

Building in public. The pipeline is open. 🚀
