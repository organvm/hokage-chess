# [MP-2] Cross-pillar content rhythm spec (when chess vs. fitness vs. bridge)

**Date:** 2026-04-26
**Status:** PROPOSED
**Related Atoms:** MP-10 (Time Budget), BR-1 (Bridge Content), MP-1 (Pillar Taxonomy)

## 1. Executive Summary

This document defines the production cadence and publishing rhythm for Rob's dual-pillar ecosystem (Hokage Chess and BODI Fitness). The primary goal is to establish a predictable, sustainable weekly schedule that accommodates both pillars, seamlessly integrates "Bridge" content, and respects the time constraints that will be defined in MP-10.

Without a production cadence, the content output lacks consistency, risking audience engagement and making cross-pollination between the two pillars nearly impossible.

## 2. Weekly Content Calendar Structure

The baseline rhythm is designed to keep both audiences engaged without overwhelming the creator or causing audience whiplash. The cadence establishes specific days for specific domains, building habitual consumption for the audience.

*   **Monday:** Chess Content (Hokage)
    *   *Focus:* Deep dives, game analysis, tactical concepts, or opening repertoire.
*   **Tuesday:** Fitness Content (BODI)
    *   *Focus:* Workout routines, nutrition tips, habit building, or fitness mindset.
*   **Wednesday:** Chess Content (Hokage)
    *   *Focus:* Lighter chess content, stream highlights, quick tips, or community games.
*   **Thursday:** Fitness Content (BODI)
    *   *Focus:* Form checks, recovery strategies, fitness Q&A, or transformation stories.
*   **Friday:** Chess Content (Hokage)
    *   *Focus:* Weekend tournament prep, interactive challenges, or casual blitz.
*   **Saturday:** Bridge Content (BR-1)
    *   *Focus:* Content that explicitly connects the two pillars. This serves as the crossover point to cross-pollinate the audiences.
*   **Sunday:** Quest Log (Newsletter Recap)
    *   *Focus:* A comprehensive weekly wrap-up sent via email and cross-posted. Summarizes the week's wins in both domains, previews the upcoming week, and offers exclusive insights for newsletter subscribers.

## 3. Monthly Rhythm & Major Beats

Beyond the tactical weekly execution, the monthly rhythm incorporates larger strategic beats to drive the business funnel:

*   **Week 1:** Goal setting and theme introduction for both pillars.
*   **Week 2:** Mid-month progress check-in (highlighted in Sunday's Quest Log).
*   **Week 3:** Deep dive and Premium content tease (pushing toward higher-tier funnel conversions).
*   **Week 4:** Monthly review, community shoutouts, and performance analysis.

## 4. Bridge Content (BR-1) Mechanics

Bridge content is the critical connective tissue preventing the two pillars from feeling disjointed to a unified audience.

*   **Cadence:** Strictly designated for Saturdays to anchor the weekend and reward the most engaged followers.
*   **Format:** Can be a long-form video, a detailed carousel, or an analytical blog post.
*   **Objective:** To introduce chess fans to the fitness methodology (and vice versa) using shared vocabulary.
*   **Examples of Bridge Themes:**
    *   "The mental stamina needed for a 4-hour chess match built through cardiovascular endurance."
    *   "How discipline in daily fitness translates directly to discipline in chess study."
    *   "Opening preparation (Chess) vs. Warm-up routines (Fitness)."

## 6. Production-Friction Estimates per Format

To sustain the cadence without burnout, we need to estimate the friction (time + mental load) for each format:

*   **Long-form Video (Deep Dives, Bridge Content)**
    *   *Friction Level:* HIGH (5-8 hours)
    *   *Process:* Scripting/Outlining -> Setup/Lighting -> A-Roll/B-Roll -> Editing -> Thumbnail -> SEO/Publish.
*   **Short-form Video (Stream Highlights, Quick Tips, Form Checks)**
    *   *Friction Level:* LOW-MEDIUM (1-2 hours)
    *   *Process:* Clip extraction / Quick recording -> Vertical edit/captions -> Publish.
*   **Written Content (Carousel, Analytical Blog Post)**
    *   *Friction Level:* MEDIUM (2-4 hours)
    *   *Process:* Research/Writing -> Graphic design/Formatting -> Review -> Publish.
*   **Live Streams (Casual Blitz, Community Games)**
    *   *Friction Level:* LOW (Requires dedicated time block, but low prep/post-production)
    *   *Process:* Setup -> Live interaction -> VOD save.
*   **Quest Log / Newsletter**
    *   *Friction Level:* MEDIUM (2-3 hours)
    *   *Process:* Weekly review/synthesis -> Copywriting -> Formatting in email client -> Proofreading.
